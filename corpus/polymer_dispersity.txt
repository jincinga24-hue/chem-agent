Polymer Molecular Weight and Dispersity

Synthetic polymers are not single molecules but distributions of chain lengths.
Two averages of this distribution matter most.

Number-average molecular weight Mn
Mn is the simple arithmetic mean of all chain weights. If a sample contains N_i chains
of weight M_i, then Mn = sum(N_i M_i) / sum(N_i). Mn is the average a colligative
property (osmotic pressure, freezing point depression) measures.

Weight-average molecular weight Mw
Mw weights each chain by its own mass: Mw = sum(N_i M_i^2) / sum(N_i M_i). Mw is what
light scattering and viscometry measure. Mw is always greater than or equal to Mn,
with equality only for a perfectly monodisperse sample.

Dispersity Do (formerly polydispersity index, PDI)
Dispersity is defined as Do = Mw / Mn. By definition Do >= 1. A Do close to 1.0
(usually 1.0 to 1.2) indicates a narrow distribution typical of living/controlled
polymerizations such as RAFT, ATRP, or anionic polymerization. Conventional free-
radical polymerization typically gives Do around 1.5 to 2.5.

Degree of polymerisation
DP_n = Mn / M_repeat_unit, the number of repeat units in an average chain.

Schulz-Flory dispersity
A free-radical polymerization with chain transfer to monomer has a most-probable
(Schulz-Flory) distribution with Do = 2 in the high-DP limit.

Dispersity in RAFT (Mueller equation)
For a controlled (RAFT, ATRP, NMP) polymerization, the dispersity at conversion x
follows the Mueller equation in simplified form:
  Do(x) = 1 + 1/DP_n(x) + (k_p / k_tr) (2 - x) / x
where k_p is the propagation rate constant and k_tr is the chain transfer rate
constant of the CTA. Equivalently, since the chain transfer constant C_tr = k_tr / k_p:
  Do(x) = 1 + 1/DP_n(x) + (1 / C_tr) (2 - x) / x
Higher C_tr gives narrower dispersity. At x close to 1 the second term tends to
1/C_tr, so Do plateaus at approximately 1 + 1/C_tr. With CDB (C_tr ~ 6000) on
styrene, the achievable dispersity is below 1.05 over a wide conversion window.

Measurement
Dispersity is measured by size-exclusion chromatography (SEC), also called gel
permeation chromatography (GPC), with a refractive-index or light-scattering detector.
Calibration standards (commonly polystyrene standards) calibrate the elution time vs
molecular weight, but the apparent Mn from SEC depends on the standard, the solvent,
and the column. Triple-detector SEC (RI + light scattering + viscometry) gives
absolute Mn and Mw without standards.

Implications for design
A narrow dispersity (Do close to 1) is desirable when uniform chain length matters,
e.g. for polymer therapeutics, block copolymers, and self-assembling materials. RAFT
gives Do typically 1.05 to 1.20 if the CTA is matched to the monomer and the recipe is
well-tuned.
